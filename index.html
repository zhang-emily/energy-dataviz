How the U.S. Generates Electricity ⚡

In the U.S., energy generation is highly geographical and often depends on what resources are readily avaiable in a given region. The "Predominant Energy Source" map shows the main energy source for generation for each state in 2019.

Toggle to view the renewable energy targets for each state. In addition to renewable-friendly geographical features such as lots of wind or sun, state policies influence the energy mix of a state. California, Massachussetts, and and North Carolina are a few examples of states where renewable energy policies are the most favorable. On the other hand, the coal-heavy states like West Virginia tend to lack these renewable energy targets.

Click on a state to view the historical energy trends, between 2000 and 2019. The energy composition of each state can differ widely. In general, the trend across the U.S. has been: decreasing coal, increasing natural gas, increasing wind and solar, while nuclear and hydro have held steady. Wind in particular has picked up rapidly in Texas and the Midwest, becoming the primary energy resource in several states.
